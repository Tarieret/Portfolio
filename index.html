Economic Time-Series Forecasting on AWS SageMaker Completed
Macroeconomic forecasting carries real consequences: poor predictions inform bad policy and investment decisions.
Benchmarked ARIMA, LSTM, and XGBoost on U.S. CPI data to find the most reliable architecture. XGBoost, paired with stationarity transformations and a 12-month lookback window, outperformed both alternatives, after pivoting away from LSTM once an architectural mismatch with the dataset scale became clear. Deployed on AWS SageMaker with a custom inference handler for scalable cloud hosting.

RAG-Based Research Assistant Completed
Domain-specific medical literature is dense and fragmented, and standard LLMs hallucinate when asked questions they haven't been grounded in.

03
House Plant Health Classification Using Amazon Rekognition Completed
Identifying plant health issues early prevents loss, but visual assessment is subjective, inconsistent, and doesn't scale.
Built an automated classification pipeline with particular attention to the minority class, where misclassification carries the highest cost, including class imbalance correction through targeted augmentation, stratified splitting, and labeled S3 uploads via manifest files. The Rekognition Custom Labels model reached 95% accuracy with 1.00 precision and 0.80 recall on the unhealthy class, meaning it's highly reliable when flagging at-risk plants.

04
Film Industry EDA (SQL & R) Completed
The entertainment industry commonly assumes popular films are also well-rated. This project stress-tested that assumption at scale.
Across 60,000+ films from a 1.3M-row TMDB dataset, using a production-style pipeline (raw data → SQLite → SQL → R/ggplot2), two findings stood out: popularity and rating are essentially uncorrelated (r = 0.087), meaning commercial success isn't a proxy for quality, and Drama consistently outrates Action (6.27 vs. 5.96), a statistically significant gap across 35,000+ films with direct implications for content investment strategy.

05
StyleSignal: Trend Lifecycle Clustering In Progress
Building a pipeline to classify trend behavior by shape rather than volume. Search-interest time series for style and brand keywords (via pytrends) are decomposed into shape-based features (peak timing, rise rate, decay rate, duration above threshold, amplitude) rather than clustering on raw interest values. Unsupervised clustering groups trends into lifecycle patterns (spike-and-fade, sustained growth, cyclical return), testing whether lifecycle shape predicts commercial relevance.
