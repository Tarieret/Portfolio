XGBoost vs. ARIMA vs. Prophet, deployed end-to-end on AWS SageMaker.

Overview

This project forecasts U.S. Consumer Price Index (CPI) values using a multi-model approach, ranging from classical statistical methods to gradient boosting. The primary objective was to evaluate model accuracy under a rigorous time-series validation framework and demonstrate production-level deployment on AWS SageMaker.

A key decision mid-project was pivoting away from LSTM after testing showed deep learning struggled with the small monthly sample size and non-stationary trend of CPI data. It was a deliberate architectural choice in favor of a more robust tree-based approach.

Technical Approach
  • Applied first-order differencing to remove trend bias: the model predicts monthly delta rather than absolute CPI values
  • Engineered a 12-month sliding window of lag features to capture seasonal momentum and annual cycles
  • Benchmarked ARIMA and Prophet as statistical baselines before training XGBoost
  • Serialized the final model into model.tar.gz with a custom inference handler for SageMaker deployment
  • Kept the deployment script intentionally separate from the notebook to avoid unnecessary cloud costs
Key Decisions
  • Pivoted away from LSTM after testing showed it struggled with the small monthly sample size and non-stationary CPI trend, choosing a more robust tree-based approach instead
  • Applied first-order differencing to predict monthly delta rather than absolute CPI values, directly addressing non-stationarity
  • Engineered a 12-month lookback window to capture seasonal and annual cycles
  • Kept the deployment script separate from the notebook to avoid unnecessary cloud costs

This project reinforced that model selection should follow the data's constraints, not the other way around. A simpler, well-regularized architecture outperformed a more complex one once the dataset's scale and non-stationarity were properly accounted for.
